## Project Title: 
Global Unemployment Market Analysis


## Objective: 
- Global events: Evaluating how major global events such as financial crises and pandemic affect unemployment rate across different Human Development Index (HDI) tiers.
- Gender Disparity: Analysing the gap and correlation between female and male unemployment rates and how they are influenced by education level. 
- Generational Gaps: Examining the unemployment difference between youth (ages 15–24) and adults (ages 25+) and identify the reasons behind the difference.

           
## Key Findings:
- Highly developed countries are more susceptible to financial crises and pandemic due to their deep connections to international trade and global financial markets. 

- Less developed countries are less vulnerable to major global events as reflected by their more stable unemployment rate across the years. 

- Countries at low education level reveal a seemingly higher female unemployment rate than male due to female's engagement in black-market jobs which are not counted into employment market. 

- Female unemployment rate is constantly higher than that of male in countries at moderate education level since females are expected to stay at home and be housewives, indicating the severity of sexism in these countries.

- In countries at high education level, the gap between female and male unemployment rate has been narrowing in recent years due to the emphasis of gender equality and feminism. 

- Highly developed countries have a higher disparity between youth and adult unemployment rate owing to higher educational requirements and robust work experience to secure a job.

- Less developed countries which rely on informal sectors and have lower legal working age have relatively lower youth unemployment rate
